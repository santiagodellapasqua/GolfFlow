Golf Hotel Guide

Golf Hotels, Golf Breaks & Golf Holidays in the UK: A Complete Guide

Golf hotels offer something that a standard golf club — however good — can never quite replicate: the ability to wake up on-site, play your first round before breakfast, and return to a luxury hotel room at the end of the day. For serious golfers, a golf hotel break is simply the best way to play.

What Is a Hotel Golf Break?

A hotel golf break combines overnight accommodation at a hotel with one or more rounds of golf at an on-site or affiliated course. Most packages are available as bed & breakfast or dinner, bed & breakfast, and include preferential tee times as part of the arrangement. At Macdonald Hotels, golf breaks can be tailored to include extra rounds, spa access, group catering and more — making them equally suitable for a solo golfer, a couple, a group of friends or a society outing.

The key advantage of a hotel golf break over a day trip is time: staying on-site means an early morning tee time is genuinely easy, there's no commute before or after, and you have the full flexibility of the course across the duration of your stay.

Golf Hotels in Scotland: The Best Destinations

Scotland is home to some of the world's most celebrated golf, and our Scottish golf hotel venues are set in landscapes that few courses elsewhere can match. Macdonald Aviemore Resort's Spey Valley Championship Golf Course plays through the Cairngorms mountain range — one of the UK's most dramatic and memorable settings for any round of golf. The nearby Macdonald Cardrona Hotel, Golf & Spa in the Scottish Borders offers a different flavour: rolling Border countryside, an 18-hole course and exceptional spa facilities to complete the stay.

A Scottish golf hotel break from Macdonald isn't just about the golf — it's about the whole experience: the Highland air, the local whisky, the scenery and the hospitality that Scotland does better than anywhere in the world.

Golf Hotels in England: Championship Courses & Luxury Stays

England's Macdonald golf venues range from the professionally designed Emerald and Sapphire courses at Hill Valley Hotel, Golf & Spa in Shropshire to the championship parkland layout at Portal Hotel & Golf Resort in Cheshire — one of the North West's finest golf hotel experiences. Our English golf hotel breaks combine quality golf with luxury accommodation, award-winning spa facilities and outstanding food and drink.

Society Golf Days: How to Organise the Perfect Golf Day

Organising a golf society day is a significant undertaking — managing tee times, catering, prizes, competition formats and accommodation for up to 100 players requires experience and attention to detail. At Macdonald Hotels, a dedicated event organiser handles all of this from the moment of your enquiry. The result is a seamless, professionally organised event that your group will want to repeat year after year.

Society golf days at Macdonald venues include flexible start time allocation, custom competition formats (stableford, medal, Texas scramble and more), half-way house and meal options, prize presentations and — for those travelling from further afield — hotel accommodation packages. Low deposits and a final payment structure not due until four weeks before arrival make the financial logistics of a society day significantly simpler.

Golf Holidays in Spain: The Costa del Sol

For UK golfers seeking sunshine and outstanding golf simultaneously, the Costa del Sol remains Europe's finest destination. With over 70 courses within easy reach of Málaga Airport and a climate that supports comfortable golf year-round, it's no surprise that Spanish golf breaks from Macdonald Resorts are so popular. Our four Costa del Sol resorts combine access to exceptional affiliated courses with resort-quality accommodation, pool facilities, spa treatments and fresh Mediterranean dining — all within a 2.5-hour flight from most UK airports.

Golf Memberships: Is a Hotel Golf Course Membership Right for You?

Golf memberships at hotel courses offer advantages that traditional private club memberships rarely can: hotel-standard facilities and changing rooms, the ability to dine and relax on-site after a round, and — for Macdonald members — exclusive discounts on hotel stays, spa treatments and food and drink across the Macdonald estate. For golfers who value quality of environment alongside their golf, a hotel club membership delivers a genuinely distinctive experience.
